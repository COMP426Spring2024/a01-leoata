Timeline

July 1985: Formation of Enron

Enron is formed by the merger of Houston Natural Gas and InterNorth.

1990: Creation of Enron Finance Corp

Jeff Skilling is hired to lead Enron Finance Corp, introducing innovative but risky financial practices.

December 2000: Peak Share Price

Enron's share price hits an all-time high of $90.75, reflecting the company's apparent success.

August 2001: CEO Resignation

Jeff Skilling abruptly resigns as CEO, citing personal reasons, with Kenneth Lay resuming the role.

October 16, 2001: Earnings Restatement

Enron announces a $618 million loss for Q3 and a $1.2 billion reduction in equity, related to partnerships managed by CFO Andrew Fastow.

October 22, 2001: SEC Investigation

The Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) begins an inquiry into Enron's accounting practices.

December 2, 2001: Bankruptcy Filing

Enron files for Chapter 11 bankruptcy, becoming the largest bankruptcy in U.S. history at that time.

January 2002: Criminal Investigation

The Department of Justice opens a criminal investigation into Enron's collapse.

October 2002: Fastow Charges

Former CFO Andrew Fastow is charged with fraud, money laundering, and conspiracy.

July 2004: Skilling and Lay Charges

Jeff Skilling and Kenneth Lay are charged with fraud and conspiracy related to Enron's collapse.

May 2006: Skilling and Lay Convictions

Skilling and Lay are convicted on multiple charges of fraud and conspiracy.

December 2006: Lay's Death and Conviction Vacated

Kenneth Lay dies of a heart attack, leading to his conviction being vacated.
